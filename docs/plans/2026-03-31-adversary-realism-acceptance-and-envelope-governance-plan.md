# Adversary Realism Acceptance And Envelope Governance Plan

**Goal:** Freeze a shared acceptance contract for the remaining adversary-realism tranches so envelope changes model real hostile personas, closure claims depend on measurable realism escalation, and later Game Loop or Tuning work cannot reopen from convenient but underpowered attacker behavior.

**Architecture:** Reuse the existing realism contract, receipts, observer read models, contributor public-site terrain, and Make-driven proof surface. Add no new simulator-only shortcuts. Tighten closure gates and backlog wording instead.

**Tech Stack:** Existing adversary-sim planner and workers, observer receipts, Makefile proof targets, roadmap and backlog docs, research and plan indexes.

**Related research:** [`../research/2026-03-31-adversary-realism-acceptance-and-envelope-governance-review.md`](../research/2026-03-31-adversary-realism-acceptance-and-envelope-governance-review.md)

## Guardrails

1. Do not accept realism work from higher ban counts alone.
2. Do not accept any realism envelope that exists only to protect the simulator or reduce pressure without a hostile-persona rationale.
3. Do not accept receipt-shape improvements alone without observable hostile escalation on the protected public surface.
4. Do not let stronger realism widen host knowledge, add hidden route catalogs, or add privileged trust-header shortcuts.
5. Do not reopen Game Loop or Tuning from narrative claims of realism; require explicit scorecard proof instead.

## Shared Acceptance Scorecard

Every remaining realism tranche must declare which of these dimensions it is expected to improve and must close only when the relevant pre/post baseline change is observable:

1. `Emission realism`
   - burst size, dwell, concurrency, request cadence, requests-per-identity, recurrence timing, or hostile action-family breadth.
2. `Terrain realism`
   - deeper public-surface reach, higher traversal counts, lower unexplained frontier remainder, or richer hint-driven discovery.
3. `Defence-engagement realism`
   - more realistic challenge, PoW, maze, honeypot, tarpit, rate-control, or retry interaction for the modeled persona.
4. `Identity and transport realism`
   - parseable trusted-ingress client IPs, pool-backed identities, coherent geo or locale posture, or deeper transport truth.
5. `Safety and truthfulness`
   - same-origin, root-started, public-hint-only, Shuma-blind behavior and no privileged worker trust shortcuts.
6. `Representativeness readiness`
   - explicit readiness or degraded-realism truth rather than optimistic claims.

## Envelope Governance Contract

Every remaining envelope-bearing realism change must include:

1. the hostile persona or campaign style being modeled,
2. the research or field evidence that justifies that operating range,
3. the scorecard dimensions expected to change,
4. the proof surface and exact `make` target or evidence path,
5. and an explanation of why the change is not merely a simulator comfort limit.

If a proposed envelope cannot answer those questions, the tranche is incomplete.
